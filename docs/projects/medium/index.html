Summary

The article discusses my personal experience of the Master of Data Science program at the University of British Columbia. It describes my initial excitement for the program, followed by the sudden realization of how challenging it is. It highlights the differences between undergraduate and graduate programs and the difficulty of the workload. In the article, I am recognizing that my experiences may not be as stressful as some of my classmates’ experiences, who are juggling full-time jobs and taking care of their children while studying. The article emphasizes that the purpose of education is not just to learn the course materials but also to train individuals to be motivated problem-solvers.
